Xiaoquan (Michael) Zhang, DaPeng Xu, Hong Hong et al.
▎English Abstract
Despite the increasing attention associated with ESG investing, a natural yet overlooked question is how investors would react to the ESG performance of firms, given that investors' activities can ultimately affect corporate ESG behaviors. Green governance aims to resolve environmental externalities at minimum cost. By uniting Coase's two foundational theories (1937, 1960) we reframe MNEs' green governance as the internalization of environmental externalities through global value chains. Venture Capital and Private M&A Contracting

风险投资与私募并购合同研究
Lauren J. Vollon
▎English Abstract
This study investigates the role of venture capital (VC) firms in private mergers and acquisitions (M&A) contracting, particularly in reducing information asymmetry. Analyzing a proprietary data set of private M&A transactions, the paper finds that VC-backed targets use more contingent consideration and fewer seller-friendly provisions, suggesting that VC involvement improves information quality in these opaque transactions.
▎中文摘要

Endogenous Commitments: Implications for Supply Chains

内生承诺：对供应链的影响
Narendra Singh
▎English Abstract
Although a centralized firm selling durable products benefits from committing to future prices, it remains unclear whether this conclusion extends to a decentralized supply chain where upstream and downstream firms interact strategically. This study demonstrates that endogenous commitment choices by supply chain members fundamentally alter pricing dynamics, inventory decisions, and overall supply chain efficiency compared to centralized benchmarks.
▎中文摘要

Outsiders at the Helm: When Institutional Outsiderness Helps or Hurts Public Leaders' Crisis Management Performance

外部人掌舵：制度性外部人身份何时帮助或损害公共领导者的危机管理绩效
Lin Jiang, Dong Liu, Yan Yu
▎English Abstract
Institutional outsiders have become increasingly common in public administrations, coinciding with a growing prevalence of public crises. Yet we know little about whether and under what conditions leaders with limited political experience perform well or poorly during such crises. Drawing on strategic leadership and crisis management theories, this study identifies boundary conditions under which outsider leaders' fresh perspectives become assets versus liabilities.
▎中文摘要
制度性外部人在公共行政领域日益普遍，恰逢公共危机频发。然而，我们对政治经验有限的领导者在此类危机中表现优劣的条件知之甚少。本研究借助战略领导力与危机管理理论，识别外部领导者新鲜视角转化为优势或劣势的边界条件。

Impact, Interrupted: How and When Thwarted Prosocial Impact Undermines Employee Performance and Retention

被打断的影响：受阻的亲社会影响如何及何时削弱员工绩效与留任意愿
Jordan D. Nielsen, Kinshuk Sharma, Daniel D. Goering
▎English Abstract
Employees are increasingly motivated by the desire to make a positive difference through their work. But when organizational barriers thwart their prosocial impact, the psychological consequences can be severe. This study develops and tests a model of how interrupted prosocial impact cascades into diminished performance and increased turnover intentions, identifying critical moderating factors that can buffer against these negative effects.
▎中文摘要
员工越来越渴望通过工作产生积极影响，但当组织障碍阻碍其亲社会影响时，心理后果可能十分严重。本研究构建并检验了一个模型，揭示受阻的亲社会影响如何连锁导致绩效下降和离职意愿上升，并识别了能够缓冲这些负面效应的关键调节因素。

Rewriting the Imprint: How #MeToo Led CEOs From Male-Dominant Cultures to Increase Gender Equality

重写烙印：#MeToo如何引导男性主导文化中的CEO提升性别平等
Michael J. Mueller, Taco H. Reus
▎English Abstract
This paper examines how CEOs' cultural imprints shape women's representation in corporate leadership and how social movements can alter these effects. Building on imprinting theory, we argue that transformative social movements, such as the #MeToo movement, can create secondary sensitive periods during which CEOs from male-dominant cultures become more receptive to gender equality initiatives, effectively "rewriting" their earlier cultural imprints.
▎中文摘要
本文考察CEO的文化烙印如何塑造女性在企业领导层中的代表性，以及社会运动如何改变这些效应。基于烙印理论，研究认为#MeToo等变革性社会运动可创造二次敏感期，在此期间来自男性主导文化的CEO对性别平等举措更加开放，有效"重写"了其早期文化烙印。

Populism, Regime Characteristics, and MNC Location Choice

民粹主义、政权特征与跨国企业区位选择
Srividya Jandhyala, Vera Kunczer, Thomas Lindner
▎English Abstract
Countries with populist governments are less attractive investment destinations for Multinational Corporations (MNCs), as foreign firms tend to be delegitimized and targeted as members of the outgroup. However, populist governments vary in the extent to which they delegitimize foreign firms. This study examines how regime characteristics moderate the negative effect of populism on MNC location choices, offering implications for global talent deployment and multinational HR strategy.
▎中文摘要
民粹主义政府执政的国家对跨国企业吸引力较低，因为外资企业往往被去合法化并作为外部群体被针对。然而，民粹主义政府在外资去合法化程度上存在差异。本研究考察政权特征如何调节民粹主义对跨国企业区位选择的负面影响，为全球人才部署和跨国人力资源战略提供启示。
